# Transition Board Blueprint (Unified 6-Zone Pitch Sheet)

*This blueprint applies the 6-Zone Unified Storyboard layout to a sequence designed to visualize a transition, transformation, or world switch. It breaks abstract concepts into visible intermediate states.*

## Zone 1: Header Row (Metadata)
- **Title/Scene**: Transformation Sequence [Insert Transition, e.g., "Human to Werewolf" or "Day to Night Timelapse"]
- **Sequence Type**: Visual Transition / Morph
- **Frame Rate / Runtime**: Varied (can be a rapid 1-second morph or a slow 10-second blend)
- **Vibe/Tone**: Surreal, magical, or seamless technical

## Zone 2: Character & Styling Lock Row
- **State A Lock**: Define the precise starting form (e.g., "Normal human in a white shirt").
- **State B Lock**: Define the precise ending form (e.g., "Massive werewolf with torn white fabric").
- **Bridge Elements**: Identify what stays exactly the same across the transition to anchor the viewer's eye (e.g., "The glowing yellow eyes remain constant").

## Zone 3: Camera & Movement Plan Zone
- **Camera Body/Lens**: Often locked off (tripod) or executing a perfectly smooth, robotic motion-control move to allow the visual effects to match perfectly.
- **Camera Flight Path**: 
  - Match cut mechanics.
  - E.g., `Camera pushes directly into the subject's eye (State A) -> Crosses the pupil threshold -> Pulls out of the eye into the new world (State B).`

## Zone 4: Expressions & Action Keyframes Zone
- Map out the microscopic details of the transformation:
  1. *The Catalyst*: The glowing light, the injected serum, the snap of a finger.
  2. *The In-Between*: Skin turning to fur, or a modern wall dissolving into ancient bricks.
  3. *The Anchor*: The one element (a hand, a prop) that connects State A to State B during the morph.

## Zone 5: Core Storyboard Grid (Sequential Panels)
*(Focus on visualizing the intermediate states. NO direct jumps from A to B.)*

- **PANEL 1**: Before State (前状态)
  - *ACTION*: Establish the baseline reality or starting form.
- **PANEL 2**: Trigger Point (触发点)
  - *ACTION*: Show the exact moment or action that initiates the transition.
- **PANEL 3**: Transition Phase 1 (中间态1)
  - *ACTION*: Show the beginning of the transformation (e.g., lighting shifts, geometry starting to warp, element blending).
- **PANEL 4**: Transition Phase 2 (中间态2)
  - *ACTION*: Show the peak of the transformation, bridging the old and new states.
- **PANEL 5**: After State (后状态)
  - *ACTION*: Reveal the newly established reality or form.
- **PANEL 6**: Stable Final Frame (稳定尾帧)
  - *ACTION*: Allow the viewer to settle on the new state.

## Zone 6: Technical Specs Footer
- **Lighting**: Use lighting transitions to help sell the morph (e.g., a bright flash of light covering the geometry change, or a shadow wiping across the frame).
- **Materials/VFX**: Morphing topology, particle dispersion, dissolving pixels, magical auras.
- **Color Script**: A stark contrast between State A's palette and State B's palette, with the transition panels showing a literal mixing of the colors.
